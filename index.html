Research interests

My present research interests lie in the topics that fall under the umbrella of Robust, Interpretable and Secure Human Centered Computing. I work in the area of robust systems for social signal processing using multimodal deep learning techniques (conversation analysis, natural language understanding and speech processing) to the above problems. I am primarily interested in understanding what causes machine learning models to fail or perform differently than expected, how these predictions can be explained, how the models can be secure/private in presence of malicious attacks and how user expectations frame their perception of a model output. I am also interested in applications and ethical concerns of such modeling to medical areas, especially mental health. You can find out more about my research here.

In past, I have worked on mental health prediction using social media posts as proxy (B.E. Thesis). I have also worked on multimodal deception detection (ICDM-W 2016) and predicting empathy in human behavior (FLAIRS 2017).
